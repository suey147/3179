AI Index

In our rapidly evolving world, Artificial Intelligence (AI) has emerged as a driving force behind economic growth and innovation. The Global AI Index (GAII) is an indicator created by Tortoise Media to rank countries based on the capacity for AI, specifically by measuring investment, innovation, and implementation levels. This global AI race has captivated nations, pushing them to the forefront of technology and economic development. To navigate this landscape effectively, it's crucial to identify the countries leading the charge in AI progress.

AI Implementation

Implementation metrics represent the application of artificial intelligence by professionals in various sectors, such as businesses, governments, and communities. The implementation factors group may be divided into three smaller groups - Talent, Infrastructure, and Operating Environment.


Infrastructure

The reliability and scale of access Infrastructure, from electribity and internet, to super computing capabilities.


With an average score of 63.5, this chart highlights the varying degrees of infrastructure development worldwide. Notably, China, Hong Kong, and Luxembourg emerge as the top three countries in terms of infrastructure, showcasing their exceptional advancements in this domain.


Operating Environment

The regulatory context, and public opinion surrounding artificial intelligence


With an average score of 66.93, this chart illuminates the diverse landscapes within which businesses and industries operate globally. Notably, Saudi Arabia, Poland, and Mexico emerge as the top three countries, showcasing their robust and conducive operating environments.



Talent

The availability of skilled practioners for the provision of artificial intelligen solutions.


With an average score of 16.8, indicating the general level of talent and expertise available in the surveyed countries. With the United States of America, India, and the United Kingdom leading the chart, it's evident that these nations possess a significant pool of skilled professionals and experts in the field of artificial intelligence. However, the average score suggests room for improvement in nurturing talent across the board. Enhancing educational programs, investing in research and development, and fostering innovation ecosystems can further elevate the talent pool in these countries.

AI Investment

Investment metrics reflect financial and procedural commitments to artificial intelligence. Tortoise Media divides these metrics into two groups of indexes - Government Strategy and Commercial.


Commercial

The level of startup activity, investment and business initiatives based on artificial intelligence.


In the Commercial category, the average score of 6.17 highlights the challenges faced by countries globally in commercializing artificial intelligence technologies. Despite the significant advancements in the United States of Americas (100) and notable efforts in China and South Korea, most countries in the dataset struggle to achieve substantial progress in this area. The comparatively low average underscores the complexities of translating AI research and development into commercially viable products and services on a large scale.


Government Strategy

The depth of commitment from the national government to artificial intelligence; investigationg spending commitments and national strategies.


In the Government Strategies category, the average score of 57.87 highlights the varying approaches and initiatives adopted by countries in shaping their AI policies and strategies. Canada, China, and Saudi Arabia lead the pack with robust government strategies aimed at harnessing artificial intelligence for societal and economic development. The wide spread in scores across countries underscores the diversity in policy frameworks and the different levels of emphasis placed on AI in various nations. This disparity reflects the complexity of crafting effective government strategies in response to the rapid advancements in AI technology.


Ranking

On the global AI map, two formidable power players, the United States and China, dominate the field. However, China's AI prowess is nearly 40% weaker than that of the USA. Following closely are the United Kingdom and Canada, both considered traditional champions in the AI arena. Beyond these established players, a group of rising stars is emerging, including Israel, Singapore, South Korea, and the Netherlands. These nations, although currently not as powerful as the USA and China, exhibit significant potential and rapid advancements, positioning them as contenders in the evolving landscape of artificial intelligence.


relationship


While many countries boast robust AI infrastructure and government policies that encourage AI growth, the shortage of skilled AI talent poses a significant challenge. This scarcity hampers innovation and development efforts, resulting in a gap between the theoretical potential and the actual progress in the AI sector. Additionally, the lack of adequate investments and under-commercialization in AI further complicates the landscape. Addressing these discrepancies will be crucial for nations aiming to harness the full potential of artificial intelligence, emphasizing the need for strategic initiatives in education, research, and investment to bridge these gaps and ensure sustainable AI advancement globally.
